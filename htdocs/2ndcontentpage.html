DIFFERENT GENDER IDENTITIES AND EXPRESSION

What is Gender Identity?

Gender identity is an individual's self-conception as a man or woman, a boy or girl, a mix of man/boy and woman/girl, a person who fluctuates between man/boy and woman/girl, or a person who doesn't fit into any of those categories at all. It is distinct from biological sex, which refers to the persons assigned gender at birth.


The Most Common Types of Gender Identities

Nonbinary

The word "nonbinary" refers to people who do not define themselves or their genders as "man" or "woman." These experiences are sometimes referred to in a variety of ways, including nonbinary and genderqueer.

Cisgender

A person whose gender identity fits with the sex they were assigned at birth is referred to as cisgender, or simply cis.

Genderfluid

Genderfluid is a term used to describe a person whose gender identity and presentation fluctuate/shifts between or outside of society's gender standards.

Transgender

This is an umbrella term that refers to anyone who experiences and identifies as a different gender from the gender assigned to them at birth.

Bigender

A person who identifies as bigender has two genders.



What is Gender Expression?

Gender expression refers to how a person expresses or portrays their gender in public. Clothing, hairstyle, make-up, body language, and voice are all examples of outer appearance and behavior. The choice of a person's name and pronoun also expresses gender.



Examples of Gender Expression

Masculine

having a set of attributes, behaviors, and roles typically associated with men

Feminine

having a set of attributes, behaviors, and roles typically associated with women

Androgynous

having a set of attributes, behaviors, and roles typically associated with men and women or none of both.
